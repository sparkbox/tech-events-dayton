Dayton Drone User Group

The Dayton OH Drone User Group (DUG) is an organization for amateur and professional drone users that seeks to promote the responsible use of flying robots for community service, artistic, entrepreneurial, and recreational purposes. All experience levels are welcome. We get together as a community to share tips, experiences, and of course to do some flying. We work to educate ourselves about how to build and use drones in a safe and respectful manner and to educate the public about the positive role aerial robotics can play in our society. Most importantly, we think that flying robots are a lot of fun! Dayton OH DUG is a member of the Drone User Group Network, an association of community organizations that promote the use of civilian drone technology for the benefit of humanity.

Dayton Cyber Security Meetup

This is a group for anyone interested in cyber security research. We will hold monthly events to discuss unconventional and creative blackhat hacking techniques and the implementation of defensive countermeasures. This meetup strives to take a proactive approach to cyber security through full disclosure of attack methodology by conducting proof-of-concept technical demonstrations and holding open discussion to raise awareness.

Dayton Dynamic Languages User Group

The Dayton Dynamic Language User Group is primarily focused on, as you can tell by the name, dynamic languages. If you're interested in Ruby (including Rails), Python (including Django), PHP, Perl, Javascript, Scheme, Lisp, Smalltalk, etc, this is probably the place for you. We try not to talk specifically about things like C and Java, but there are no hard and fast rules (especially if you're comparing them to a dynamic language).
